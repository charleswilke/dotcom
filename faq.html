What do you think about all of this AI stuff? ▼
GenAI's encroachment on the creative process has not been comfortable.

I see the ire and desire to be compensated for our creativity extracted as training data. I believe in the data dignity model proposed by Jaron Lanier. If our creative contributions are accessed when generating an output, we deserve payment.

We should be incentivized to contribute in a way that directly benefits others.

What is your process? ▼
It's never really the same thing twice.

I often start with a draft, something tangible, if not a little disjointed. Then I'll load that draft as context into a conversation with the latest, greatest AI chat interface. Then I bounce around in discussion over the core nugget in my draft.

Often, I'll request a revised draft after that conversation. From there I edit, tweak, adjust, add imagery and upload.
How much of this is really even you? ▼
Nothing appears here that I haven't specifically intended to be seen. This includes all opinions and choice in subject matter.

I also highlight AI generated passages with bold and italic that remain intact after my edit. And all imagery presented on this site is generated by me.

I am editor in chief with all things that appear on this site. I just happen to pay ChatGPT and Claude 20 bucks a month to be my writing partners.
How do you balance your own creativity with AI? ▼
I've heard it said that the internet will soon be awash in B+ content thanks to Gen AI. So these tools allow everyone to create content that anyone would consider good enough. But there will always be a huge chasm between good and great - meaningless and meaningful - inert and provocative.

That's where artistry can be found - attempting to bridge those gaps.
